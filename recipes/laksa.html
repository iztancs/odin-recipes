Laksa

Description

Laksa is a spicy noodle dish popular in Southeast Asia.

Laksa consists of various types of noodles, most commonly thick rice noodles, with toppings such as chicken, prawn or fish.

Most variations of laksa are prepared with a rich and spicy coconut soup or a broth seasoned with sour asam (tamarind or gelugur).

Originating from Peranakan cuisine, laksa recipes are commonly served in Singapore, Indonesia, and Malaysia.

Ingredients

Sambal

  • 10 fresh red chiles (100g)
  • 10 stalks dried chile (20g), soaked
  • 2 teaspoon belacan (shrimp paste)
  • 6 teaspoons cooking oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar

Spice Paste

  • 10 shallots, peeled and sliced
  • 3-inch segment old turmeric (30g), skinned
  • 2-inch segment galangal (60g)
  • 8 stalks lemongrass (100g), sliced
  • 20 stalks dried chiles, soaked until softened
  • 10 candlenuts (60g)

Broth

  • 1/2 cooking oil
  • 1 quart prawn or chicken stock
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 20 pieces tofu puffs, scalded in hot water briefly to remove oil
  • 2 teaspoon salt, or to taste

Assemble

  • Cooked egg noodles
  • Cooked vermicelli
  • 200g bean sprouts, blanched
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 500g prawns, boiled and peeled
  • 2-3 large fishcakes, boiled and sliced
  • 20 quail eggs, hard-boiled (see notes)
  • 1 bunch polygonum leaves, or daun kesum, finely sliced (see notes)

Steps

  1. Blanch noodles in boiling water and drain.
  2. Place some noodles into a bowl, garnish with bean sprouts and cucumber.
  3. >Ladle hot laksa broth and tofu puffs over noodles.
  4. Top with prawns, fish cake slices, quail eggs, and daun kesum (see notes) if using.
  5. Serve with sambal.
